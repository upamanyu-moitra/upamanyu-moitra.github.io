Hi! Welcome to the (yet another) webpage of Upamanyu Moitra.

I am a researcher in theoretical physics working on string theory, gravity, quantum field theory and quantum information, with a broad interest across disciplines and in uncovering structural parallels in diverse complex systems. My research focuses on developing quantitative frameworks to address challenging, often open-ended, problems, with an emphasis on identifying patterns, building models and extracting robust and interpretable insights. This has led to a body of work including multiple peer-reviewed publications, ranging from collaborative projects to single-author contributions, that have been recognized and widely used in the field.
 
Alongside my research, I work extensively with analytical and computational methods, including mathematical modeling, statistical simulation and data-driven approaches. I enjoy exploring new techniques and quickly adapting them to different problem settings. I have presented my work at international conferences and leading institutions, including Harvard University. I have been actively involved in mentoring, teaching and organizing research activities. I value clear communication, collaborative problem-solving and bringing creative perspectives to technically demanding questions.
